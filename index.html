// DAY 02 · APRIL 06, 2026

Tech Industry
Company Visits

Our first day of industry immersion — exploring how local and global tech companies operate in the Philippine context.

MORNING

HYTEC POWER INC.

A leading renewable energy technology company in the Philippines. We learned about sustainable power solutions, solar energy systems, and the future of clean energy in Southeast Asia.

Renewable Energy Solar Power Innovation
  • The Philippines has vast untapped solar energy potential, especially in provinces like Zamboanga.
  • Renewable energy is not just about saving the planet — it's a massive economic opportunity.
  • HYTEC employs local engineers trained in cutting-edge energy technologies.
AFTERNOON

OPEN TEXT

A global leader in Enterprise Information Management (EIM) software. Their Manila office handles large-scale software development and tech support for global clients.

Enterprise Software Cloud Solutions Global IT
  • Filipino software developers are competing globally with world-class technical skill.
  • Enterprise solutions require not just coding skills but also strong communication and project management.
  • The IT sector is one of the fastest-growing industries offering high-salary careers in the Philippines.
PHOTOS · DAY 2
HYTEC Power

OpenText Office
OpenText Office

Seeing the offices of HYTEC Power and OpenText was an eye-opener. These aren't just workplaces — they're ecosystems of innovation. The engineers we met were passionate about their work, and it made me realize that a career in technology isn't just about writing code; it's about solving real-world problems at scale.

// DAY 03 · APRIL 07, 2026

Creative Studio
& BPO Giants

MORNING

TOP PEG ANIMATION & CREATIVE STUDIO, INC.

One of the Philippines' premier animation studios, producing world-class 2D and 3D animation for international and local clients.

2D Animation 3D Rendering Creative Arts
  • Filipino animators are producing content for global streaming platforms and movie studios.
  • Animation is a technical craft requiring mastery of both artistic skills and software tools.
  • The creative industry is a viable and growing career path in the Philippines.
AFTERNOON

TELEPERFORMANCE PHILIPPINES INC.

The world's largest customer experience management company. The Philippines is one of their biggest hubs, employing tens of thousands of Filipinos.

BPO Customer Experience Global Operations
  • The BPO industry contributes significantly to the Philippine economy, with billions in annual revenue.
  • Strong communication skills and empathy are just as important as technical knowledge in this field.
  • Teleperformance uses AI tools to augment human agents — the future of CX is hybrid.

Top Peg showed us that art and technology are inseparable. Every frame of animation is a technical achievement. Teleperformance, on the other hand, reminded us that the most advanced technology still needs the human touch — understanding, patience, and communication are irreplaceable skills.

// DAY 04 · APRIL 08, 2026

Government Tech
& BGC Night

MORNING

MMDA COMMAND CENTER

The Metropolitan Manila Development Authority's nerve center — managing traffic, disasters, and public safety for 17 cities using real-time data systems.

Smart City IoT
AFTERNOON

MICROSOURCING PHILIPPINES

Australia's largest offshore staffing provider in the Philippines. They connect Australian businesses with Filipino professionals in a managed office environment.

Outsourcing Remote Work
EVENING

BONIFACIO GLOBAL CITY

A walk through BGC's modern urban landscape — a planned, walkable city district that is home to dozens of multinational companies, restaurants, and galleries.

Urban Planning Nightlife
The MMDA Command Center was like watching a city's brain in action. Dozens of screens monitored every major road, intersection, and flood-prone area in real time. We saw how technology — cameras, sensors, and data analytics — can be harnessed to manage urban chaos. It showed us that computer science and engineering degrees can be applied to public service and city governance. Smart cities aren't just a concept — Metro Manila is already implementing pieces of it.
Microsourcing showed us the global dimension of the Philippine workforce. Filipino professionals are working for Australian, American, and European companies without leaving the country. It emphasized that developing English proficiency, digital literacy, and specialized skills can open doors to international-caliber jobs right here at home. The office environment was incredibly professional and collaborative.

The MMDA Command Center made me see technology differently — not just as apps and websites, but as critical public infrastructure. Then BGC at night showed us a glimpse of what a well-planned Filipino city can look like. The contrast between these experiences is exactly why education through experience is so powerful.

// REFLECTIONS & LEARNINGS

What We Took
Home With Us

Beyond the photographs and the pasalubong, here are the lessons and insights that will shape who we become as future professionals.

Technology is Everywhere

From MMDA's traffic systems to HYTEC's solar panels, we saw how IT permeates every sector — government, energy, media, and business. The demand for tech skills is universal.

Filipino Excellence is Real

At every company, we met brilliant Filipinos excelling at the highest levels — animators, software engineers, energy specialists, and city planners. The talent is here. We need to nurture it.

History Grounds Us

Walking through Intramuros and Fort Santiago reminded us that our freedoms and opportunities were hard-won. Understanding our past makes us more intentional about the future we build.

Soft Skills Matter Most

Every company emphasized communication, teamwork, and adaptability over raw technical skills. Being a good professional means being a good human first.

Nature Restores the Mind

The mountains of Baguio and the views of Tagaytay reminded us that amid all the tech and hustle, nature is essential for our mental health and perspective.

Purpose Drives Excellence

From the PMA cadets to the animators at Top Peg, everyone we met was driven by something bigger than themselves. Knowing your "why" transforms good work into great work.

// FINAL REFLECTION
"We left Zamboanga as students.
We returned as something more."

Eight days. Two cities. Six world-class companies. Countless historical landmarks. And one group of students who will never see the Philippines — or their futures — the same way again. This was not just a tour. It was a transformation.
